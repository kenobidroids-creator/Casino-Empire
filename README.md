# Casino Empire — New Player Guide

---

## What Is Casino Empire?

Casino Empire is a top-down casino management idle game. You start with an empty floor and $5,000. Your job is to place machines and furniture, attract patrons, collect revenue, pay your staff, and grow from a single slot machine into a sprawling full-service casino.

The game runs in real time. Patrons walk in on their own, play machines, eat at the bar, and leave — all without you doing anything. Your role is making smart placement and upgrade decisions, handling jackpots and cashier payouts when they come up, and reinvesting your profits wisely.

---

## The Core Loop

```
Place machines → Patrons walk in → Patrons play & spend → 
Collect revenue → Pay wages at day end → 
Reinvest profits → Upgrade / expand → Repeat
```

Every in-game day lasts **4 real minutes at 1× speed** (2 min at 2×, 90 sec at 3×). At day end you see a full profit & loss summary and wages are automatically deducted.

## Starting Out: Your First 5 Days

### Day 1 — Get Something On the Floor

You start with $5,000. The single most important thing on Day 1 is getting revenue flowing.

**Recommended first buy:** 2–3 Basic Slots ($200 each) and a Cashier Window ($280).

Why the Cashier Window immediately? Patrons who win tickets need somewhere to cash out. Without it, winners just leave — and eventually patrons learn to avoid your floor. It's a $280 investment that unlocks your entire revenue chain.

Place slots near the centre of the floor so patrons don't have to walk far. Place the Cashier Window near the entrance so winners don't cross the whole room to cash out.

### Day 2 — Watch the Flow

Before spending more, watch how patrons behave for a full day:

- Are they clustering around certain machines and ignoring others? Placement matters — patrons prefer machines they can reach easily.
- Are jackpots flashing but nobody is paying them? That means patrons are winning big and leaving without collecting — you're losing money. Tap jackpot machines immediately when they flash.
- Is the cashier queue backing up? Consider a Ticket Kiosk ($450) as overflow.

### Day 3 — First Upgrade or Second Machine Type

By Day 3 you should have a profit. Spend it on one of:

- **Silver Slot** ($500) — higher base bet, better returns per tile
- **Upgrade a Basic Slot** — Speed, Luck, or High Stakes (see Upgrades section)
- **Bar & Grill** ($600) — adds a second revenue stream from drinks/food

### Day 4–5 — Hire Your First Employee

Once you have 4+ machines, manual management becomes the bottleneck. Your first hire should almost always be a **Slot Attendant** ($500 hire, $100/day wage) — they handle machine maintenance and reduce breakdown frequency, freeing you to focus on jackpots and cashier payouts.

## All Placeable Machines

### Slot Machines

The backbone of your revenue. All slots work the same way — patrons sit, spin, occasionally win tickets, and leave when their mood or money runs out.

| Machine | Cost | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Basic Slot | $200 | Low bet, low return. Good starter filler. |
| Silver Slot | $500 | Mid-tier. Best value per dollar in early game. |
| Gold Slot | $1,200 | High bet, attracts wealthier patrons. |
| Diamond Slot | $2,500 | Premium. Best return per tile. High maintenance. |

**Slot tips:**
- Higher tier slots attract higher-spending patron types.
- Leave at least one tile gap between slots — patrons need walking room.
- Machines break down over time. Tap a broken machine (shown with a ⚠ flash) to repair it.

### Cashier Window
**Cost: $280 | Size: 2×1**

Patrons with winning tickets walk here to cash out. You (or a hired Cashier Staff) pay the ticket. Paying exact change is slightly more profitable than auto-paying. The till shows you the ticket amount — tap the correct coin/note buttons or hit Auto-Pay.

Without a cashier, winners cannot collect. They leave frustrated, and word spreads — patron flow drops.

### Ticket Kiosk
**Cost: $450 | Size: 1×1**

An automated cashier. Patrons self-serve — no staff or player interaction needed. Slower than a staffed cashier window but runs 24/7 without input. Good as overflow or on the far side of a large floor.

### Bar & Grill
**Cost: $600 | Size: 2×1**

Adds food and drink revenue. Patrons who are happy and have money will stop at the bar. Drinks and food are fulfilled by a Food Server employee — without staff, the bar runs very slowly and patrons may leave unfulfilled orders.

**Menu items and prices:**
- Water: $4 | Beer: $8 | Coffee: $6
- Cocktail: $14 | Nachos: $12 | Burger: $16

The bar is secondary income. Don't buy it before you have stable slot revenue, but once you do it's a meaningful boost — especially on weekends when patron counts are higher.

### Surveillance Room
**Cost: $900 | Size: 1×1**

Tap it to open the Surveillance panel — a live overhead view of your floor with camera feeds. The main use is spotting **Dropped Money**: coins patrons occasionally drop. You can see them on the surveillance feed and then go claim them on the floor by swiping across them.

Also gives you oversight of the whole floor without scrolling around manually.

### Security Desk
**Cost: $500 | Size: 1×1**

Handles **Lost & Found**. Occasionally patrons lose items and a returning visitor comes back to claim them at the Security Desk. You choose whether to return the item (patron loyalty) or pocket it (quick cash, slight reputation hit). Also deters patron misbehaviour on the floor.

### TV Screen
**Cost: $400 | Size: 1×1**

Boosts mood of nearby patrons (within ~4.5 tiles) and slows departing patrons, giving them more time to spend. Pure environment upgrade — no interaction needed. Place near high-traffic zones.

### Band Stage
**Cost: $1,200 | Size: 2×2**

Live entertainment. Patrons near the stage are happier and linger longer. Best placed in the centre of the floor or near the bar. Requires a reasonable open area — it's 2×2 tiles and needs walkable space around it.

### Sportsbook
**Cost: $2,000 | Size: 2×1**

Lets patrons place sports bets. Auto-resolves on a timer. High cost but adds an additional revenue stream that runs independently of slot play. Worth it in the mid-game when you have diverse patron types.

### Table Games (Blackjack, Roulette, Poker Table)
**Cost: $3,000–$4,000 | Size: 2×2**

High-end revenue sources that attract premium patrons. The house runs the tables automatically, but you can also **play the tables yourself** — tap any table to open the player game mode:

- **Blackjack** — Beat the dealer to 21. Hit, Stand, or Double Down.
- **Roulette** — Pick a bet type (Red/Black, Even/Odd, Straight number at 35:1) and spin.
- **Poker** — Texas Hold'em style through Preflop → Flop → Turn → River.

Table game money flows through the same accounting as everything else. A hired **Table Dealer** will walk to occupied tables and boost their output.

## Upgrades

Tap any placed machine, then tap **Upgrade** in the panel. Each machine has three upgrade tracks, each with 3 levels:

| Upgrade | Effect | Best For |
|---------|--------|----------|
| ⚡ Speed Boost | Reels spin 20% faster per level | High-traffic machines with constant use |
| 🍀 Lucky Charm | +2% win rate per level (boosts patron happiness, slight revenue dip) | Machines that are losing patrons due to bad luck runs |
| 💸 High Stakes | Max bet +25% per level | Premium machines (Gold, Diamond) |

**General upgrade priority:**
1. Speed on your busiest Basic/Silver slots first — more spins = more revenue.
2. High Stakes on Gold/Diamond slots when you can afford them.
3. Lucky Charm last — it slightly reduces your edge but keeps morale up.

You can also **Move** machines without penalty — useful when you want to reorganise the floor as it grows.

## Employees

Hire from the hotbar (bottom of screen, right side). Wages are deducted automatically at each day end.

| Employee | Hire Cost | Daily Wage | Job |
|----------|-----------|------------|-----|
| Cashier Staff | $400 | $80 | Handles cashier window automatically |
| Slot Attendant | $500 | $100 | Maintains machines, reduces breakdowns |
| Food Server | $450 | $90 | Fulfils bar orders |
| Table Dealer | $600 | $120 | Walks to active tables, boosts output |

**When to hire:**
- **Cashier Staff** — when you catch yourself repeatedly missing cashier payouts because you're busy elsewhere.
- **Slot Attendant** — when you have 6+ machines and breakdowns are interrupting flow.
- **Food Server** — when the bar has consistent demand (4+ patrons ordering per day).
- **Table Dealer** — when you have table games placed and want passive output boosts.

**Wage warning:** Don't hire faster than your revenue supports. Each employee costs their daily wage regardless of how much work they do. Check your day-end P&L — if wages are eating more than 30% of revenue, hold off on new hires.

## Jackpots

When a patron wins a large prize, the machine flashes with a **🎉 Jackpot** indicator. **You must tap the machine** to trigger the hand-pay. If you don't tap it within the timeout window (~50 seconds), the patron leaves without collecting — which is good for your wallet short-term but bad for reputation long-term.

Jackpot strategy: Let a few small jackpots go to save money early. Pay big jackpots every time — the patron loyalty is worth more than the payout cost.

---

## Dropped Money

Patrons occasionally drop coins on the floor. To collect them:
- **Swipe/drag** across coins directly on the floor canvas, or
- Open **Surveillance** and identify where they are, then go collect

At day end, any uncollected dropped money appears in a summary panel — you can choose to keep it or return it (affects reputation).

---

## Lost & Found

Occasionally patrons lose items. A returning visitor will appear and walk to the **Security Desk**. You get a choice:

- **Return it** — patron is loyal, slight reputation boost
- **Keep it** — quick cash, minor reputation cost

Over many days, consistently returning items builds a reputation bonus that increases patron frequency.

---

## The Day Summary

At the end of each day you see a full P&L breakdown:

- **Revenue** (money collected from slots, bar, tables, cashier payouts in)
- **Payouts** (jackpots and ticket payments out)
- **Wages** (all employees, paid automatically)
- **Net** (what you actually made)

Use this to spot problems:
- Payouts suspiciously low? You might be missing jackpots.
- Wages eating your margin? Time to pause hiring.
- Revenue flat for 3 days? Consider a floor reorganisation or new machine type.

---

## Game Speed

The speed buttons (1×, 2×, 3×) in the top-right control how fast the day runs:
- **1×** — 4 real minutes per day. Good for learning and active management.
- **2×** — 2 minutes per day. Good for mid-game when the floor is established.
- **3×** — 90 seconds per day. Good for grinding through days when the system is humming.

The game also saves offline progress — if you close the tab and come back, you'll see an idle earnings summary.

## Strategies

### Early Game: The Spine Strategy
Place machines in a single row down the centre of the floor with clear walking lanes on each side. Patrons navigate better, and you can add branches later. Avoid clustering everything in a corner.

### Mid Game: Zone Your Floor
Separate your floor into zones — a slot section, a table games section, a bar/lounge area. Patrons tend to stick to zones once they find what they like. TV Screens near each zone help keep everyone happy longer.

### Regarding the Cashier
Never let your cashier queue get longer than 3 people. Patrons waiting more than ~15 seconds in queue often leave. Add a Kiosk on the opposite side of the floor early, before you think you need it.

### The Upgrade Order
Upgrade one machine to Level 3 Speed before spreading upgrades across many machines. A maxed-out Silver Slot outperforms three un-upgraded ones in most scenarios.

### Surveillance Pays for Itself
The $900 Surveillance Room sounds expensive early, but on a medium floor it can spot $30–80 in dropped money per day that you'd otherwise miss. It pays off within 2–3 weeks of in-game time.

### Weekend Peaks
Patron counts are higher on in-game weekends. Plan to have fresh stock, repaired machines, and the bar fully staffed before a Friday — don't spend your whole bankroll on upgrades Thursday evening.

## Testing & Feedback Guide

If you're testing the game and want to give useful feedback, here's what to look for:

**Economy balance:**
- Are you comfortably profitable by Day 5? (Should be.)
- Do wages ever make you go negative? (Probably shouldn't happen before Day 10.)
- Does any machine feel useless at its price point?

**Patron behaviour:**
- Do patrons ever get stuck or walk in circles? Note where.
- Do patrons seem to avoid certain areas even when machines are free?
- Does the cashier queue ever reach 5+ people? That indicates a spawn rate vs. cashier throughput mismatch.

**Jackpots:**
- Does the jackpot flash feel urgent enough? Easy to miss?
- Does paying jackpots feel rewarding, or just like a chore?

**Table games (player mode):**
- Does the blackjack/roulette/poker feel fair and readable?
- Are card values and hand results clearly communicated?

**General feel:**
- Does anything feel too slow or too fast?
- Is there a point where you run out of things to buy and just wait?
